     <h1>Prepositions of place</h1>

     <p>The sentences in this multiple-choice exercise are missing prepositions that denote location/place/position. Choose the most appropriate answer for each sentence. </p><p>Remember that some prepositions are always used in the dative case. For e.g, aus, von, nach, zu, etc. </p><p><b>Aus</b> means "out of" and von means "from." You would use "aus" when you want to denote nationality or where you belong. For e.g. <i>Ich komme <strong>aus</strong> Chine</i> means that <i>I hail/come from China.</i> </p><p><b>Von</b> is used when you want to say that you are directly coming from some place. For e.g., <i>Ich komme <strong>von</strong> der Arbeit</i> literally means that you are coming from the work. </p><p>"Nach" and "zu", both mean "to." However, <strong>nach</strong> is used with reference to geographic locations or directions; <strong>zu</strong> is used in all other cases except these. Also, <strong>zu</strong> is always used with an article or combined and contracted with the article that follows it. For e.g. <i><strong>zu dem</strong> Bahnhof</i> becomes <i><strong>zum</strong> Bahnhof.</i></p>
